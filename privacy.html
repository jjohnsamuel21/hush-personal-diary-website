Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Effective immediately

← Back to Hush

🔒 The short version: your private diary entries are AES-256-GCM encrypted on your device and never transmitted to any server. We do not collect, sell, or share your personal writing. Ever.

1. Who we are

Hush is a personal diary application for Android, built and maintained as an independent project. This privacy policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and how it is protected. By using Hush, you agree to the practices described here.

2. Private diary entries — fully on-device

All private diary entries you create in Hush are:

  • Encrypted on your device using AES-256-GCM before being written to local storage
  • Never uploaded, transmitted, or synced to any server — by design
  • Only decryptable on your device using a key derived from your PIN/biometric
  • Completely inaccessible to us, even if we wanted to read them

This architecture is intentional. Your private thoughts are private — not as a policy choice, but as a technical guarantee.

3. Shared notes — what goes to our servers

Hush includes an optional Shared Notes feature that allows you to co-edit notes with other users in real time. This feature requires Google Sign-In and transmits data to our backend. Specifically, when you use Shared Notes:

  • Your Google account name, email address, and profile photo URL are stored on our servers
  • The content of notes you explicitly share is stored in our database (PostgreSQL on Railway)
  • Invitations, collaborator relationships, and permissions are stored server-side
  • Real-time edits are transmitted over a secured WebSocket connection

Shared Notes are entirely opt-in. If you never use the Shared tab or sign in with Google, no data of any kind leaves your device.

4. Authentication data

If you sign in with Google to use Shared Notes, we receive an ID token from Google which we verify server-side. We store:

  • Your Google account ID (a numeric identifier, not your password)
  • Your email address
  • Your display name and profile photo URL (used to show your avatar to collaborators)

We do not store your Google password. Authentication is handled entirely through Google's OAuth 2.0 infrastructure.

5. Local data storage

All private diary entries, journal structure, settings, and preferences are stored in a local SQLite database on your device. This data:

  • Remains entirely on your device at all times
  • Is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM
  • Can be exported as an encrypted backup and imported on any device running Hush
  • Is deleted permanently when you uninstall the app

6. Analytics & tracking

Hush contains no analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising SDKs, and no third-party tracking of any kind. We do not use Firebase Analytics, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or any equivalent service. The app makes no network requests except:

  • Google Sign-In authentication (Shared Notes only, when you initiate it)
  • API calls to our backend for Shared Notes CRUD operations
  • WebSocket connections for real-time shared note collaboration
  • GIPHY API calls when you search for GIFs to embed in a note

7. Third-party services

The following third-party services are used, under these limited conditions:

  • Google Sign-In — only when you authenticate for Shared Notes. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • GIPHY — only when you search for GIFs within the editor. Your search query is sent to GIPHY's API. Subject to GIPHY's Privacy Policy.
  • Railway (hosting) — our backend is hosted on Railway's infrastructure. Shared note data transits through Railway's servers.

8. Data retention

  • Private diary data: stored only on your device; deleted when you uninstall
  • Shared notes data: retained on our servers until you delete the note or your account
  • Account data: retained until you request deletion

To request deletion of your account and all associated shared notes data, contact us at the email address below. We will process deletion within 14 days.

9. Children's privacy

Hush is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through Shared Notes, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

11. Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or your data, please open an issue on our GitHub repository or reach out via GitHub Discussions.

Your diary is yours. We built Hush to be a tool that works for you, not a product that extracts value from you. If you ever have a concern about your data, ask us directly.
